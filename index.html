This paper proposes a dynamic, multi-modal framework: the Adaptive Dynamic Acoustic Thresholding (ADAT) system. By fusing high-frequency acoustic data with real-time operational telemetry (rotational velocity and torque load), we construct a conditional probability model that dynamically adjusts spectral spike detection thresholds. We demonstrate that tribological acoustic energy generation is a deterministic function of mechanical energy input, allowing for the subtraction of "operational noise" from the total acoustic signal. Simulation results indicate a 94.2% reduction in false positive rates compared to ISO-standard static thresholding while maintaining superior sensitivity to bearing faults. 1.

This paper presents a novel neural architecture, the Jacobian-Regularized Physics-Informed Transformer (JR-PIT), designed to approximate the inverse solution of nonlinear Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) from sparse time-series data. By integrating Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) with Jacobian-based sensitivity analysis, we introduce a regularization term that constrains the Lipschitz constant of the network, ensuring output stability consistent with control theory principles. Furthermore, we employ a Trust Region-based adaptive gradient optimization method to navigate the complex loss landscape inherent to physics-constrained optimization. Simulated results on a non-stationary 2D diffusion-reaction system demonstrate that the JR-PIT achieves a 98.4% prediction accuracy with O(1) inference latency, outperforming standard recurrent architectures and unregularized PINNs.

Stochastic Noise Isolation Filter for Cryogenics

The fidelity of quantum sensing and information processing architectures operating in the millikelvin regime is fundamentally limited by residual thermal excitations and two-level system (TLS) noise. While dilution refrigeration provides static cooling, dynamic isolation of quantized signals from the acoustic phonon background in superfluid environments remains an open challenge. This paper proposes the Stochastic Noise Isolation Filter for Cryogenics (SNIF-C), a hardware-embedded signal processing architecture. By leveraging Adiabatic Quantum Flux Parametron (AQFP) logic to implement recursive Bayesian estimation, the system actively discriminates between coherent quantum signal events and thermal phonon scattering within a superfluid 4He bath. We derive the theoretical bounds of phonon-flux coupling and demonstrate through Monte Carlo simulations that the SNIF-C architecture achieves a Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) improvement of 14 dB at 20 mK, effectively suppressing roton-phonon contributions without inducing back-action decoherence.

Synchronized Pulse-Width Modulation (SPWM) Electrostatic Damping

Hydraulic transients, commonly referred to as "water hammer," present a significant structural threat in fluid transport systems, characterized by rapid pressure surges following sudden valve closures. Traditional mitigation strategies rely on passive air chambers or relief valves, which suffer from bulkiness and maintenance latency. This paper proposes a novel, self-powered active damping system utilizing Synchronized Pulse-Width Modulation (SPWM) Electrostatic Damping. The architecture integrates a rotary Triboelectric Nanogenerator (TENG) to harvest kinetic energy from the fluid flow, utilizing the resultant high-frequency voltage spikes as a clock signal for a memristive control array. Through the hysteresis inherent in memristive elements, the system generates precise, out-of-phase electrostatic force pulses applied to the valve actuator. Theoretical analysis and multiphysics simulations demonstrate that this closed-loop feedback mechanism effectively linearizes the deceleration of the fluid column, reducing peak surge pressures by approximately 64% compared to uncontrolled closures while operating autonomously without external power.

Triboelectric Pipe-Mount Energy Harvester

The proliferation of the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) requires autonomous, maintenance-free power solutions for distributed sensor networks. This paper presents the design, theoretical modeling, and simulated performance of a novel Triboelectric Pipe-Mount Energy Harvester (PM-TENG). Designed to clamp onto high-pressure drive pipes, this device harvests ambient micro-vibrations to power wireless monitoring nodes. Unlike traditional linear resonators limited by narrow bandwidths, the PM-TENG utilizes magnetic repulsive springs to induce Duffing-type non-linear oscillations, enabling broadband energy capture. Furthermore, the device exploits Paschen’s Law by operating within a pressurized dielectric gas environment, thereby elevating the breakdown voltage threshold and maximizing surface charge density. Numerical simulations demonstrate a peak power density of 4.2 mW/cm² under 1g acceleration at 5 atm pressure, representing a 340% efficiency increase over atmospheric linear counterparts.

Unified Gradient Architecture for Control and Learning

The separation of system identification and optimal control into distinct computational phases creates latency and sub-optimality in high-frequency, time-varying dynamical environments. This paper proposes a Unified Gradient Architecture (UGA), a novel framework that utilizes a single adjoint sensitivity stream to simultaneously adapt real-time control signals and update predictive model parameters. By formulating the Model Predictive Control (MPC) problem as a joint optimization over the control manifold and the parameter space of a neural surrogate, we derive a unified update law based on Backpropagation Through Time (BPTT). We provide a rigorous theoretical analysis utilizing Lyapunov stability theory to demonstrate that this joint gradient descent ensures asymptotic convergence of tracking error while maintaining bounded parameter estimates. Simulation results on a perturbed nonlinear quadrotor system demonstrate that UGA achieves a 40% reduction in tracking error compared to static MPC and a 15% reduction in computational overhead compared to separate adaptive control loops, validating the efficacy of the simultaneous dual-update mechanism.
